Down below is a dosa recipe

Description

Dosa is a staple South Indian crepe made from a fermented batter of ground rice and black gram (urad dal). It is prized for its unique texture, featuring a lacy, golden-brown crispness on the outside and a slightly soft, tangy interior. Traditionally cooked on a flat griddle, it is often served alongside spicy sambar and a variety of fresh coconut chutneys. While the plain version is a classic, the "Masala Dosa" filled with a spiced potato mash is one of the most popular variations worldwide. Rich in carbohydrates and proteins due to the fermentation process, it is a versatile dish enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • lentils
  • oil

  • Fresh veggies
  • Spices and Salt
  • Water

Steps

  1. Rinse and Soak: In one bowl, rinse 3 cups of parboiled rice and 1 cup of raw rice. In a separate bowl, rinse 1 cup of urad dal and 1 tsp of fenugreek seeds. Soak both in plenty of water for 4 to 6 hours.
  2. Drain: After soaking, drain the excess water, but keep a small cup of the soaking water asideâ€”it contains wild yeast that helps with fermentation.
  3. Grind the Dal: Using a wet grinder or a high-speed blender, grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds with a little chilled water until the paste is fluffy, light, and smooth. Transfer to a large pot.
  4. Grind the Rice: Grind the rice with just enough water to create a slightly grainy consistency (like fine semolina).
  5. Mix: Combine the rice and dal pastes in the pot. Add a teaspoon of salt and mix thoroughly with your hands (the natural heat and bacteria from your hands actually jumpstart the fermentation!).
  6. Rest: Cover the pot and leave it in a warm, dark place for 8 to 12 hours. The batter is ready when it has nearly doubled in volume and smells slightly tangy.
  7. Heat the Tawa: Heat a flat, non-stick or cast-iron griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease it with a few drops of oil and wipe it off.
  8. The Swirl: Pour a ladleful of batter in the center. Starting from the middle, use the back of the ladle to spiral the batter outward in a circular motion until you have a thin, even crepe.
  9. Crisp: Drizzle a teaspoon of oil or ghee around the edges. Cook until the bottom turns golden brown and the edges start to lift.
  10. Fold: Once crispy, fold the dosa in half or roll it into a cylinder. There is usually no need to flip it, as the thin batter cooks through quickly.
